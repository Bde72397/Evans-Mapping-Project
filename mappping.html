Continuing Education for Children in Transitional Housing

- Brian Evans

December 11 2020






Homeless advocates are worried about the future.

“New York City was already grappling with a historic housing and homelessness crisis before the pandemic – nearly 60,000 people sleep in the main municipal shelter system each night,” said Jaquelyn Simone, a policy analyst at the Coalition for the Homeless.

Instead, advocates like Simone fear children being the new crisis in homelessness.

The chart above outlines several resources in the South Bronx: Homebase locations, drop-in centers - which provides immediate assistance without prior request - and their accompanying location within the community districts.

A recent article in Politico echoed these advocates’ fears. 35% of families seeking eligibility for homeless services and housing originate in the Bronx. In total, five of the 10 communities throughout New York City that have recorded the highest rates of homelessness come from the Bronx – including Morris Heights and the South Bronx.
